## 02 · The real gap
phase: SOURCE
duration: 12

SAY:
Modern AI tools have gotten good at persistence. Claude has projects. ChatGPT has memory. They can recall your preferences, your writing style, even your past conversations. That's real progress. But here's what none of them give you. A computer that's always on. A place where your AI can store files, hold credentials, run code, and act on a schedule — even when you're asleep. The gap isn't memory. The gap is infrastructure. Think about it this way. You can have the smartest employee in the world, but if they don't have a desk, a laptop, access to your tools, and a schedule to show up — they're just someone you talk to sometimes. That's what most AI is right now. Brilliant. But homeless.

## 03 · What an agent actually needs
phase: SOURCE
duration: 10

SAY:
An agent isn't a smarter chatbot. An agent is a model with infrastructure. It needs a computer — a real server that stays on. It needs storage — files that persist between conversations, between days, between months. It needs credentials — API keys, login tokens, access to your Gmail, your calendar, your Slack. It needs a schedule — the ability to wake up at seven thirty AM and do something without you asking. And it needs a way to reach you — SMS, email, Telegram, Slack, whatever channel you already live in. Without those things, you have a very smart conversation partner. With those things, you have something that can actually work for you.

## 06 · The arc
phase: SOURCE

SAY:
